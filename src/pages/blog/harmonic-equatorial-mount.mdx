<div slot="section-a" class="section-intro">
    <h1>2-Axis Harmonic Equatorial Mount DIY Process</h1>
    <p>
        For deep-sky astrophotography, tracking accuracy is paramount. Standard worm-gear equatorial mounts require heavy counterweights and are prone to backlash. This project documents the complete engineering cycle of a custom DIY equatorial telescope mount powered by strain wave (harmonic) gear systems on both Right Ascension (RA) and Declination (DEC) axes.
    </p>
    <p>
        By eliminating traditional counterweights and utilizing high-torque strain wave actuators, the system achieves a payload-to-weight ratio of 3:1. Designed in CAD, CNC machined from 6061-T6 aluminum, and powered by an OnStep open-source controller, this telescope mount delivers sub-arcsecond tracking precision.
    </p>

        Scroll down to interactively explore the mechanical design components and internal gear structures of this high-precision astronomical equipment.
    </p>

<div slot="section-c" class="section-conclusion">
    <h2>Performance Analysis & Verification</h2>
    <p>
        The mount underwent extensive tests using a 80mm f/6 refractor telescope and a ZWO ASI294MC imaging camera. Guide performance was monitored via PHD2 autoguiding software. Periodic error, backlash response, and structural stiffness were mapped during multiple clear sky tracking sessions.
    </p>
    
    <p>
        Backlash in both axes is virtually non-existent, owing to the zero-backlash characteristics of the industrial strain wave gears. Periodic error was measured at +/- 8 arcseconds over a 400-second period, which is easily compensated to less than 0.5 arcseconds RMS using PHD2's Predictive PEC (Periodic Error Correction) algorithm.
    </p>
